PID correction factor calculator (draft)

Beta — verify before relying on this. Uses correction factors from the ERG air monitoring table. Assumes a typical PID calibrated to isobutylene equivalents and applies the usual RAE-style rule: implied true ppm ≈ reading ÷ correction factor (see your instrument manual; conventions vary). A single reading does not identify which vapor is present — each row is “if the response were from this compound alone.” Mixtures, humidity, and lamp condition are not modeled.
